# Caribbean Currencies Pegged to the US Dollar

If you're sending money to the Caribbean, understanding which currencies are **pegged to the US dollar** can help you predict exchange rates and potentially save money on transfers.

A currency peg means the local currency maintains a fixed exchange rate against the US dollar. This stability can make your transfers more predictable — you'll know exactly how much your recipient will receive.

## Quick Reference: USD-Pegged Caribbean Currencies

| Currency | Code | Peg Rate | Countries |
|----------|------|----------|-----------|
| Bahamian Dollar | BSD | 1:1 | Bahamas |
| Bermudian Dollar | BMD | 1:1 | Bermuda |
| Barbados Dollar | BBD | 2:1 | Barbados |
| Belize Dollar | BZD | 2:1 | Belize |
| Eastern Caribbean Dollar | XCD | 2.70:1 | 8 island nations |
| Aruba Florin | AWG | 1.79:1 | Aruba |

## Currencies Pegged at 1:1 (Equal to USD)

### Bahamian Dollar (BSD)
The Bahamas maintains a **1:1 peg** with the US dollar. In practice, US dollars are accepted everywhere in the Bahamas alongside BSD. This makes transfers straightforward — $100 USD = $100 BSD.

### Bermudian Dollar (BMD)
Bermuda also maintains a **1:1 peg**, and US dollars circulate freely alongside the local currency. Most businesses accept both interchangeably.

## Currencies Pegged at 2:1

### Barbados Dollar (BBD)
Since 1975, the Barbados dollar has been pegged at **$2 BBD = $1 USD**. This rate has remained stable for nearly 50 years, making it highly predictable for transfers.

**What this means for you:** If you send $500 USD, your recipient receives approximately $1,000 BBD (minus any transfer fees and margins).

### Belize Dollar (BZD)
Belize maintains a similar **2:1 peg** with the US dollar. This fixed rate has been stable since 1978.

## Eastern Caribbean Dollar (XCD) — 8 Countries

The Eastern Caribbean Dollar is used by **eight island nations** and is pegged at **$2.70 XCD = $1 USD**. This rate has been fixed since 1976.

### Countries Using XCD:
1. 🇦🇬 **Antigua & Barbuda**
2. 🇩🇲 **Dominica**
3. 🇬🇩 **Grenada**
4. 🇰🇳 **Saint Kitts & Nevis**
5. 🇱🇨 **Saint Lucia**
6. 🇻🇨 **Saint Vincent & the Grenadines**
7. 🇦🇮 **Anguilla**
8. 🇲🇸 **Montserrat**

The XCD is managed by the **Eastern Caribbean Central Bank (ECCB)**, which ensures the peg remains stable across all member nations.

**Note:** The British Virgin Islands are ECCB members but use the US dollar directly.

## Non-Pegged Caribbean Currencies

Not all Caribbean currencies are pegged. These major currencies **float** against the US dollar:

| Currency | Code | Type | Notes |
|----------|------|------|-------|
| Jamaican Dollar | JMD | Floating | Can fluctuate significantly |
| Trinidad & Tobago Dollar | TTD | Managed float | More stable than JMD |
| Guyanese Dollar | GYD | Managed float | Recently more volatile |
| Haitian Gourde | HTG | Floating | High volatility |

For floating currencies, exchange rates change daily. This means:
- You might get a better rate one day vs another
- Comparing providers on the day of transfer is essential
- Consider rate alerts to catch favorable movements

## Why Currency Pegs Matter for Money Transfers

### Predictability
With pegged currencies, you know exactly what your recipient will get (minus fees). A $500 transfer to Barbados will always convert to ~$1,000 BBD.

### Lower Spreads
Transfer providers often charge smaller exchange rate margins on pegged currencies because there's less risk. You may find better rates sending to Barbados (pegged) vs Jamaica (floating).

### Timing Doesn't Matter (Much)
For pegged currencies, waiting for a "better rate" doesn't make sense — the rate is fixed. Focus on comparing transfer fees instead.

## FAQ

### Do US dollars work in these countries?
In countries with 1:1 pegs (Bahamas, Bermuda), US dollars are widely accepted. In other pegged countries (Barbados, XCD nations), some tourist businesses accept USD but you'll often get a worse rate.

### Can the peg change?
Currency pegs can technically be abandoned, but Caribbean pegs have been remarkably stable. Barbados has maintained its peg since 1975, and the XCD peg has held since 1976.

### Should I send USD or local currency?
For pegged currencies, it usually doesn't matter much. Compare the total amount received in either currency option offered by your transfer provider.
