## The Rate That Matters: Mid-Market vs. What You Actually Get

There are two rates worth knowing:

**The mid-market rate** — also called the interbank rate or the "real" exchange rate. This is the midpoint between the buy and sell price of a currency. It's what Google shows. It's what XE.com shows. No provider will match it exactly, but the best ones come close.

**The retail rate** — what you actually get. Every provider adds a margin on top of the mid-market rate. Some are transparent about it (0.4% clearly stated). Others don't mention it at all, advertising "no fees" while quietly giving you a rate 4–5% worse than mid-market.

The difference matters. On £500, a 4% margin costs you BBD 53. On £1,000, it costs you BBD 107. On £2,000 — BBD 214 gone before your recipient sees a penny.

## Why the GBP/BBD Rate Is Simpler Than You Think

Here's something most comparison sites don't tell you: **the Barbadian dollar is permanently pegged to the US dollar at exactly 2:1.**

One USD = two BBD. Always. That rate has not moved since 1975 and is fixed by law.

This means the GBP/BBD exchange rate simply tracks the GBP/USD rate, multiplied by two. When the pound strengthens against the dollar, your recipient gets more BBD. When it weakens, they get less.

**Current rates (9 March 2026):**
- Mid-market GBP/USD: £1 = $1.3358
- Mid-market GBP/BBD: £1 = BBD 2.6715 (= $1.3358 × 2)

Every provider in the table below is working from this same base rate. The gap between what you see and what they give you is their margin.

Most providers charge an extra 0.3–1% for debit card funding. Bank transfer is always the cheapest payment method, just slightly slower (1–3 hours to process).

Revolut applies a 0.5% weekend surcharge. If your transfer isn't urgent, wait until Monday morning.

**5. The bigger the transfer, the more the rate matters**  
On £100, a 4% vs 0.5% margin difference is BBD 9 — annoying but minor. On £2,000, that's BBD 70+ — worth spending 2 minutes to compare.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What is the GBP to BBD exchange rate today?**  
As of 9 March 2026, the mid-market rate is £1 = BBD 2.6715. This changes daily with GBP/USD movements. Check our [live rate widget](/send-money) for the current figure.

**Why is the BBD rate fixed?**  
The Barbadian dollar has been pegged to the US dollar at exactly 2:1 since 1975. It's maintained by the Central Bank of Barbados. This means GBP/BBD simply tracks GBP/USD multiplied by two.

**Which provider gives the best GBP to BBD exchange rate?**  
TransferGo and Wise consistently offer the best rates for UK to Barbados transfers — both within 0.5% of the mid-market rate. TransferGo edges ahead on rate on most amounts; Wise leads on transparency and reliability.

**Is the exchange rate the same for all transfer amounts?**  
The margin percentage is typically consistent, but fixed fees have more impact on smaller transfers. On £100, a £2 fee is 2% of your total. On £1,000, it's 0.2%. Larger transfers benefit proportionally more from choosing a low-fee provider.

**What was the GBP to BBD exchange rate historically?**  
Since BBD tracks USD at 2:1, the historical GBP/BBD rate follows GBP/USD history. At GBP/USD highs (around $1.70 in 2014), £1 bought BBD 3.40. At post-Brexit lows ($1.07 in 2022), £1 bought just BBD 2.14. The current rate of ~BBD 2.67 is in the mid-range of the past decade.

**Can I lock in today's exchange rate for a future transfer?**  
Some providers (XE Money, OFX) offer forward contracts — locking a rate for up to 12 months. Wise and TransferGo do not. Forward contracts make sense if you're regularly sending large amounts and want certainty over rate fluctuations.
